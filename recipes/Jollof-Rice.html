Jollof-Rice

Description

Jollof Rice is a flavorful and popular West African one-pot dish made with long-grain parboiled rice cooked in a tomato-based sauce. The rice is infused with a delightful blend of spices, including paprika, thyme, and curry powder, giving it a vibrant red color and a rich aroma. Jollof Rice is often prepared with vegetables, meat, or seafood, making it a hearty and satisfying meal. It is a cherished and iconic dish in many West African countries, each region adding its unique twist to the recipe. Enjoyed on various occasions, Jollof Rice is a true celebration of African culinary culture and a favorite dish at gatherings and family feasts.

ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cups tomato puree or blended tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps.

  1. a. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear, then drain and set aside. b. In a large pot or saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. c. Add the chopped onions, garlic, red bell pepper, and green bell pepper. Saut√© until the onions become translucent. d. Stir in the tomato puree, tomato paste, paprika, ground thyme, curry powder, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook the tomato base for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  2. Cook the Jollof Rice Ingredients: Prepared tomato base 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth Cooked chicken, beef, or shrimp (optional) Instructions: a. Add the parboiled rice to the tomato base and stir until the rice is well-coated with the sauce. b. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and add the cooked chicken, beef, or shrimp if using. Stir to combine. c. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let the rice simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  3. Fluff and Serve Instructions: a. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat. b. Gently fluff the Jollof Rice with a fork to separate the grains. c. Serve hot as a delicious main dish or alongside grilled meat, fish, or fried plantains
